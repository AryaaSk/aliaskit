## Contingency Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Demo Crashes at 10:15 AM
**Response:**
1. Product team immediately diagnoses and starts recovery
2. Growth Marketer posts to Twitter/HN: "Issue with demo, stand by"
3. Devrel posts to Discord: "Brief technical issue, should be resolved in X minutes"
4. Pause social media amplification for 15 minutes
5. Resume once demo is back online

**Success Metric:** Demo is back up within 30 minutes

### Scenario 2: Support Email Overload (100+ inquiries in first hour)
**Response:**
1. Ops team implements support queue protocol
2. Growth Marketing acknowledges high volume in Discord/Twitter
3. Provide FAQ link and self-serve resources
4. Escalate complex issues to product team
5. Set expectation: "Support may take 1-2 hours due to high volume"

**Success Metric:** All support emails receive response within 2 hours

### Scenario 3: Negative Sentiment on HackerNews
**Response:**
1. Product team diagnoses technical concern (if applicable)
2. Growth Marketer responds thoughtfully to criticism
3. Don't be defensive; engage authentically
4. Share feedback with product team for follow-up
5. If criticism is valid, acknowledge and commit to fix

**Success Metric:** Tone improves by Day 2 or issue is addressed

### Scenario 4: Traffic Surge Exceeds Capacity (>500 signups in 1 hour)
**Response:**
1. Product team activates scaling protocol
2. Database team monitors for performance issues
3. Consider temporary rate limiting if necessary
4. Growth Marketer announces: "Wow! We're overwhelmed with demand. Ramping up capacity."
5. Celebrate the win, not the problem

**Success Metric:** Servers stay up, conversion rate holds above 15%

### Scenario 5: Key Team Member Goes Down
**Response:**
1. Designate backup from same team
2. Knowledge transfer on critical tasks
3. Adjust workload priority
4. Escalate to CTO/CMO if impact is severe

**Success Metric:** Launch proceeds with <5 min delay
