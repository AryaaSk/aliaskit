## March 29 Launch — Reality Check

### What Needs to Happen TODAY:

**9:00 AM:** All-hands call (30 min)
**9:30 AM:** Parallel function reviews (90 min)
- [ ] CEO: Review messaging + pitch (30 min)
- [ ] Product: Verify technical claims (45 min)
- [ ] Marketing: Confirm resource availability (45 min)
- [ ] Legal: Review press + investor materials (30 min)
- [ ] Communications: Assess press kit feasibility (30 min)
- [ ] Ops: Confirm website deployment (30 min)

**11:00 AM:** Consolidation (30 min)
- Collect all approvals or changes
- Make 1-2 hour edits if needed
- Final sign-off

**By 12:00 PM:** CEO confirms "All systems go"

**By 5:00 PM:** All changes implemented, team prepped for tomorrow

### What Happens Tomorrow:

**6:00 AM:** Final readiness check (30 min)

**8:00 AM:** LAUNCH
- Website live
- Press release sent
- Email announcement sent
- Social posts go live

**10:00 AM:** Discord announcements start
**2:00 PM:** Quality checks
**5:00 PM:** End-of-day momentum report
**6:00 PM:** HackerNews submission

**All day:** Real-time monitoring and engagement

### Risks:

1. **Approval delay:** One function takes too long → cascading delays
2. **Website not ready:** If Ops confirms deployment at 12:00 PM, that's cutting it very close
3. **Legal pushback:** If Legal finds compliance issues, no time to fix
4. **Changes needed:** If Product spots technical inaccuracies, no time for revisions
5. **Team fatigue:** 18-hour sprint starting TOMORROW; team exhaustion by end of week
6. **No contingency time:** If something breaks on launch day, minimal fallback options

### Why This Might Still Be Right:

- **First-mover advantage:** Launch before competitors can respond
- **Press momentum:** Launch surprise can amplify media coverage
- **Market timing:** If competitors are announcing in May, beat them to market
- **Team motivation:** Intense sprint can energize team
- **Real urgency:** If business case requires March launch, the urgency is justified

## May 2026 Launch — Reality Check

### This Week:

**Today-Friday:** Function reviews (async, 30-60 min per function)
- Read assigned content at own pace
- Provide feedback via Slack when ready
- No meetings required
- Lightweight async process

**Friday EOD:** Consolidation
- Collect all feedback
- Identify any conflicts
- Publish approval status

### Weeks 2-4:

**If revisions needed:** Make changes (1-2 iteration cycles)
**Market research:** Integrate analyst findings to refine messaging
**Staging:** Load all content into systems, test, prepare

### May:

**Launch execution:** Similar to March 29 but less time pressure
**Post-launch:** Sustained engagement with team at normal pace

### Advantages:

1. ✅ **Quality focus:** Time to get messaging right
2. ✅ **Informed positioning:** Market research validates key claims
3. ✅ **Team sanity:** No all-nighter sprint; normal working pace
4. ✅ **Revision flexibility:** Changes don't cause panic
5. ✅ **Lower risk:** Measured execution reduces failure probability
6. ✅ **Investor story:** "Validated by market research" is stronger
7. ✅ **Sustainable growth:** Better foundation for long-term success

### Risks:

1. **Competitive response:** Competitors might launch in April while you're refining
2. **Market window closing:** If startup energy is short-lived, delay hurts momentum
3. **Team motivation:** Extended timeline can lose urgency/focus
4. **Scope creep:** Weeks 2-4 give time for "just one more thing" requests
5. **News cycle:** May announcement might get buried vs. March novelty

## Decision Framework

**Choose MARCH 29 if:**
- [ ] Website deployment is ready NOW (not "will be ready")
- [ ] All team members are available for full 18-hour sprint TODAY + tomorrow
- [ ] There are specific competitive, customer, or investor pressures to launch THIS WEEK
- [ ] Board/investors have mandated March launch
- [ ] You're willing to accept quality/polish risks for speed-to-market

**Choose MAY if:**
- [ ] Quality and message clarity matter more than speed
- [ ] You want market research to validate messaging before launch
- [ ] Team capacity is limited or distributed
- [ ] You prefer measured execution over sprint mode
- [ ] Investor/customer timeline allows May flexibility
- [ ] You'd rather launch strong later than weak sooner
