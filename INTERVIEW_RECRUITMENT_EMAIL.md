## Recruitment Strategy & Sequencing

### Step 1: Survey Closes (Friday 2026-04-04)
- Export all responses
- Segment by: framework, company size, pain points, NPS score (Q9)
- Score for interview qualification:
  - **Priority A (must-interview):** NPS 8-10 + relevant segment
  - **Priority B (high-value):** NPS 6-7 + enterprise/startup
  - **Priority C (maybe):** NPS 5 or indie builders with strong opinions

### Step 2: Outreach (Monday 2026-04-07)
**Send to Priority A (5-7 people):**
- Segment-specific template (indie/startup/framework)
- Offer specific time slots
- Emphasize early access + partnership potential

**Send to Priority B (5-10 people):**
- Same template
- Open time availability
- Note: "First come, first served for interview slots"

**Send to Priority C (remainder):**
- Template 4 (stay in loop)
- No interview ask, just relationship building

### Step 3: Confirm (by Wednesday 2026-04-09)
- Target: 10-15 confirmed interviews
- Schedule across Week 2-4:
  - Week 2: 5-7 interviews
  - Week 3: 3-5 interviews
  - Week 4: 2-3 interviews

### Step 4: Follow-Up
- Send Zoom link + prep guide 24 hours before
- Reminder 2 hours before
- Thank you + early access email within 24 hours of interview

## Customization Tips

**Make it Personal:**
- Reference their specific survey response: "You mentioned setup complexity is your #1 pain point..."
- Call out their framework/company: "We loved your [Anthropic SDK] setup..."
- Note their segment: "As an indie builder, you..."

**Address Their Needs:**
- Indie builders: Emphasize "no red tape" + "direct input"
- Startups: Highlight "partnership" + "scale with us"
- Frameworks: Focus on "revenue" + "community benefit"

**Time Zone Awareness:**
- Include their detected timezone (from survey)
- Offer global-friendly times (6am-6pm PT covers US + EU)
- Allow them to suggest their own time

**Tone:**
- Conversational, not corporate
- Show genuine interest in their work
- Keep it short (under 200 words for body)
